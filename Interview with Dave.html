Get to know Dave...

An interview with Dave Horstmann
South TX Association Chairperson


Q. Where are you from?
A. Born and raised in St. Louis, Missouri, moved to Houston, Texas in '97, went to college in Northwest Indiana before moving back to Houston where I stayed for the next 8 years.

Q. Where do you live now?
A. Two years ago I took a job offer in Austin, Texas from someone I met in line at a show at SXSW (major music festival in Austin). Most impulsive and best major life choice I ever made!

Q. What is your sobriety date?
A. By the grace of God, I have been clean and serene since January 9 of 2015 and for that I am truly grateful.

Q. How did you find Oxford House?
A. I first found Oxford House from a house flyer in a detox unit of a treatment center in my second go around at getting sober. An alumni of my treatment center came in a few weeks later to speak about his experience and I heard there were no house managers and that I had a say in how the house ran. I thought that was such a wild and awesome concept but didn't end up moving in. Still thought moving back into my apartment was a better idea. HA!

Q. When did you move into Oxford House?
A. It took me another year or two of experimenting, pushing the limits of my probation officer, multiple treatment centers, and sober homes (that were recommended for their tight structure) before I found my way to Oxford House. Despite my counselor and family's strong opinions on where I should go, I looked back to that day I saw that flyer and chose Oxford House...because it was different! The cycle I was going through wasn't working anymore. I had to try something different. So I moved into Oxford in February of 2015.

Q. What was your first Oxford House?
A. My first house was OH Spellman in Houston, Texas which was, by far, the strongest house I have ever lived in and exactly what I needed at that time. It had all the structure and support I needed but with freedom and I had a voice! I learned so much about the Oxford model and about recovery under that roof that I still look back on today. The men I lived with were true family and I still stay in regular communication with them today. The best part is the majority of them I lived with for that 18 months are still sober!

Q. Are you known for anything special?
A. My ability to talk for 8 hours without needing a break ;-) I will say, this does come in handy for leading trainings though.

Q. What do you love most about Oxford House?
A. That it gave me a sense of purpose when I needed it most. Y'all gave me the opportunity to lead and take on certain responsibilities that no other sober living community would ever dream of doing for someone with only 90 days sober. This created a momentum that couldn't be stopped and only gets stronger today.

Q. What do you think is your biggest challenge with Oxford House?
A. Staying humble and open-minded. When given positions and titles, the addict in me works quickly to attach these things to my ego. This is where my active program of recovery comes in and recognizes it and brings my awareness to it. The second I think I have this all figured out, and no longer need to ask for help, I put my foot in my mouth and spread the wrong message. I have to remind myself, that it is totally OK and encouraged to ask for the right answers when I have even an inkling of doubt that what I am saying isn't 100% true and benefits the newcomer. I've been in Oxford three and a half years and still learn new things regularly.

Q. What project are you working on now?
A. While I just recently moved out of Oxford House as a resident and straight into Austin's Alumni Association, I know the importance of staying plugged in for long term recovery. I am fulfilling my duty and responsibility to the TXSRA by finishing my term but have recently increased my efforts on a new project that I am beyond proud of. One of my absolute best friends and I were approached in February to take over the lease of a building that rented space for recovery meetings and what happened next was nothing but God acting in our lives. We began June 1st by inviting 3 dear friends and incredibly strong Oxford House residents to form a non-profit with us and build on this opportunity. Paying out of pocket for the first month just to make it possible, with no exact end date known to us, we were blessed to have the opportunity to merge our efforts with another established organization in Austin and expand on our dream to a level we never knew would be imaginable. We now sit on the board of an amazing RCO and manage what will undoubtedly become the most vibrant and fun Recovery Community Center in the great state of Texas!

Q. What was your favorite project and why?
A. Though I have been involved in a vast spectrum of different projects in my time in Oxford House from leading a planning committee for a large scale annual volleyball tournament fundraiser and many others, assisting planning unity events Statewide including the State Retreat, planning logistics for getting as many people as possible to the World Convention each year, assisting coordination of chapter splits in multiple growing cities, and more hours leading trainings across the State of Texas than I could ever count, my absolute favorite project has to be my experience of opening OH Bell! It took a lot of hard work, dedication, attention to detail, and most importantly empowering every newcomer that walked through the door, but nothing compares to the natural high that comes from building a significant and sustainable house for 12 men to call home while they begin their journey into recovery!

Q. Why do you want to join the Oxford House World Council?
A. I promised myself a long time ago, that so long as I lived in Oxford House, and was taking a bed from another addict or alcoholic who was trying to get sober, that I would give everything I had to not only help create more beds for newcomers but also to teach and uphold the model that saved my life. Like all of us, my time in Oxford House is short compared to the length of time from where it started to where it will go and I want to have the biggest impact that I can on strengthening it in the time I am here. I feel that I can help spread the incredible strengths we have in Texas with the rest of Oxford House worldwide and help save countless more lives and I am blessed to have that opportunity as an Alumni.

Q. What do you want the community to know about you?
A. That nothing can ever put out this flame or my dedication to do anything and everything in my ability to continue to serve the organization that gave me purpose and truly saved my life!
